Resume

It may be crazy but I am actually more than just a swimmer

Although Swimming has taken up the majority of my life, especially during the school year, during summers I have taken various jobs that have provided me with experience, connections, and helped me create core values of hard work, excellence and effectiveness. Below, I share some of my prior occupations and some of the things that I learned along with past achievments outside of swim.

  • Construction site manager
    • I worked for Anthony Bocchino and Solis Home Rentals in St. George, UT
    • Job entailed picking up unused material, trash, and keeping the job site clean and organized
    • I learned how a jobsite works and how to stay safe on site. I learned that hydration is key when working in the heat. I also learned how to make my money work for me.
  • Sports Camp Counselor
    • I worked for Brigham Young University in Provo, UT
    • Job entailed spending a week with a group of kids and helping them have the best spiritual, physical, and fun experience that they can
    • I learned how to discipline kids, organize chaos, manage a schedule, and make a boring experience into a good one.
  • Grounds Keeper
    • I worked for the Missionary Training Center in Provo, UT
    • Job entailed managing the landscaping for the MTC. This included cutting the grass, trimming the trees, planting the new flowers for each season, cultivating the tree rings, and other outdoor jobs.
    • I learned how to work as part of a team which meant sometimes I would pick up slack and occasionally getting my slack picked up. I learned how to plant a flower bed, how to clock in and out, and how to make my work the best it can be.
  • Valet Parker
    • I worked for Las Vegas Parkinc Co in Las Vegas, NV
    • Job entailed working for multiple casinos and resorts along the strip. I would park and retrieve cars for residents or guests at the locations
    • I learned how to talk to people and form fast connections, I learned how to work for tips, how to be efficient, and occasionally how to work under high stress situations.
  • Summer Swim Coach
    • I worked for Southern Utah Swimming Association in St. George, UT
    • The job entailed planning practices for two groups of age-group swimmers during the summer. I also needed to make sure they had fun.
    • I learned how to really care about others and their success, how to be excited even when they weren't, and I learned the extent of the human body
